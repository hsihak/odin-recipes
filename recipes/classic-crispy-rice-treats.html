Classic Rice Krispy Treats

Description

Have you ran out of Kellogg's Rice Krispies but don't want to go to grocery store to get it? Well, you can bake it yourself just under 1 hour!!!

Ingredients

  • Nonstick coopy spray
  • 4 tablespoons of unsalted butter
  • One 10-ounce bag of mini marshmallows
  • 6 cups crispy rice cereal, such as Rice Krispies
  • 1 teaspoon of pure vanilla extract
  • A pinch of kosher salt
  • Decorative topping (1/4 cup of chocolate chip or M&M candy coated chocolates while it's warm).

Steps

  1. Line a 9-by-13-inch baking dish with aluminum foil, leaving a 2-inch overhang on each of the longer sides. Spray the foil lightly with cooking spray.
  2. Melt the butter in a large pot over medium heat. Add the marshmallows and cook, stirring occasionally, until melted and smooth, about 5 minutes. Remove from the heat and stir in the vanilla and salt.
  3. Working quickly, add the rice cereal to the pot and stir with a rubber spatula until evenly coated. Transfer to the prepared baking dish and press into an even, compact layer. (Spray your hands with a little cooking spray to keep them from sticking when pressing the cereal mixture into the pan). Decorate with toppings if using (see Cook's Note).
  4. Let sit at room temperature until firm, about 30 minutes. Cut into 24 squares. Store the cereal treats at room temperature in an airtight container for up to 3 days.
